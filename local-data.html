Waste Production Trends

Key Observations

  • Winchester produces approximately 5000 tons of waste annually
  • Household waste has by 3% over the past 5 years
  • Seasonal fluctuations show peaks during spring (April-May) and late fall (October-November)
  • Per capita waste production is slightly above the state average

Ecological Significance

These waste production patterns contribute to environmental pressures, particularly in sensitive areas like the Aberjona River watershed and Horn Pond, where runoff and landfill leachate have been linked to ecosystem stress. Understanding these trends aids in identifying priority areas for waste reduction education and intervention.

Recycling and Diversion Rates

Recycling Progress

Winchester's recycling rate stands at approximately 44%, which represents slight improvement compared to previous years. The data shows:

  • Paper/cardboard and metals have the highest recyling rates
  • Plastics have among the lowest recyling rates
  • The contamination rate in Winchester's recycling stream is approximately 8%, meaning 8% of all recycled material is not recyclable

Ecological modeling suggests that improving recycling rates by just 10% could significantly reduce pressure on local landfills and decrease pollution incidents in nearby water bodies.

Environmental Monitoring Data

Water Quality Metrics

Water quality monitoring in Winchester indicates the following:

  • pH levels: Ranged from 8.0 to 9.9 (normal range: 6.5–8.5)
  • Dissolved oxygen: Specific data not available for Winchester; however, Massachusetts standards require levels above 5 mg/L for aquatic life
  • Turbidity: 0.03–0.18 NTU (well below the 0.3 NTU limit)
  • Microplastics: No specific data available for Winchester; regional studies indicate presence in nearby water bodies

Source: MWRA 2023 Winchester Water Quality Report
